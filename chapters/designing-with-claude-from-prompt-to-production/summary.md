# Summary: Designing with Claude: From prompt to production

Chapter slug: `designing-with-claude-from-prompt-to-production`

## Abstract

This talk describes the development process of Claude Design at Anthropic Labs, detailing how a three-person team built and launched a visual design collaboration product in ten weeks. Dan Carey explains the Labs methodology of running small, rapid experiments with 50-100 iteration cycles, emphasizing prototype-driven development over traditional documentation. The team optimized their entire development loop (user feedback, feature design, code shipping, feedback analysis) by leveraging AI tools and maintaining minimal coordination overhead. Key insights include replacing PRDs with conversational prototypes fed to Claude, building internal tooling within hours rather than waiting for external solutions, and focusing on hints of magic rather than fully-working initial products. The approach resulted in shipping 62 improvements within the first weekend after launch. The methodology emphasizes speed of iteration over prediction, user feedback integration at every step, and leveraging model improvements rather than over-engineering solutions to current limitations.

## Key claims

- A three-person team can build and launch a complete product in ten weeks using AI-assisted development
- Prototypes are more effective than PRDs because documents are imprecise while prototypes are concrete and visceral
- Model improvements often solve engineering problems better than clever engineering solutions
- Teams can achieve 24-hour feature turnaround times by optimizing their development loop
- Small teams with dissolved role boundaries can move faster than large specialized teams
- Daily user feedback and shipping cycles enable 50-100 iteration loops per project
- Internal tooling can be built in an afternoon rather than waiting for external solutions
