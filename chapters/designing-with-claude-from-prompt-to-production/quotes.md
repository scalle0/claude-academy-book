# Notable quotes: Designing with Claude: From prompt to production

Quotes selected by Claude from the transcript.

> My favorite thing to talk to users about is please complain at me. And my favorite thing to talk to researchers about is what have you been surprised by lately.

*Captures the core philosophy of Labs for identifying new opportunities through user pain points and research surprises.*

> We do not try to predict the future. Instead, we try to ship. We watch people use the stuff. We learn what they do. We ship. We watch. We learn.

*Articulates the fundamental methodology difference from traditional labs that try to predict versus iterate rapidly.*

> Claude Code made engineers really, really fast. And the rest of us had to keep up. So, product development timelines that used to be six months, and then they'd be a month. And then a week. And now a day.

*Demonstrates the dramatic acceleration in development timelines that drove the need for Claude Design.*

> Documents are imprecise. It's so easy for two people to look at the same doc and have two different products in mind about what the experience should be.

*Provides the core argument against PRDs in favor of prototype-driven development.*

> Everyone on the team does everything. The engineers talk to users. PMs write code. Designers do data analysis.

*Describes the dissolved role boundaries that enable small teams to move quickly.*

> You do not want to work on the thing that already works. You often want to prototype the thing, but you don't want to work on the thing that almost works.

*Counterintuitive insight about when to rely on model improvements versus engineering solutions.*

> Claude design is shipped on a Friday and by the following Monday we had shipped 62 improvements to the product.

*Quantifies the extreme iteration speed possible with their methodology.*

> Early on, you're just looking for that hint of magic. You're not looking for something that works in full, that works completely, that handles every edge case.

*Defines the threshold for pursuing a labs bet - seeking promise rather than perfection.*

> Take one real feature request from a user and turn it around in 24 hours. Get the idea in front of them and follow up with them for feedback.

*Provides a concrete challenge for audiences to identify bottlenecks in their development process.*

> The model releases are a tide that lifts all boats. They do a lot for you.

*Captures the strategic insight about leveraging model improvements rather than over-engineering current limitations.*
