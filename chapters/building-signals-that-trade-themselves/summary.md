# Summary: Building signals that trade themselves

Chapter slug: `building-signals-that-trade-themselves`

## Abstract

This talk presents Man Group's implementation of AI-powered systematic trading through Claude skills governance. Fernando describes how the $200 billion asset manager developed a framework for AI to autonomously research, backtest, and productionize trading signals. The core challenge addressed is teaching AI institutional workflows and domain knowledge through skills rather than model retraining. Initially, Man Group faced adoption issues where power users created localized optimizations instead of organizational solutions, leading to inconsistent backtesting results. The solution involved implementing skills governance with clear ownership, testing, and lifecycle management. This approach enabled AI to leverage decades of institutional knowledge for systematic trading across thousands of securities and hundreds of markets. The framework treats skills as production code with proper review processes, turning individual productivity tools into enterprise-scale capabilities. Man Group now has 750 users across departments utilizing over 100 governed skills, enabling AI agents to autonomously generate trading signals that manage real capital in production.

## Key claims

- AI can autonomously research, backtest, and productionize trading signals that manage real capital in regulated environments
- Skills governance is essential for scaling AI from individual productivity to enterprise applications
- Organizational context and institutional knowledge represent a defensible moat that frontier AI labs cannot replicate
- Power user adoption without process owner involvement leads to localized optimizations rather than organizational solutions
- Shared workflows through governed skills eliminate inconsistencies in backtesting and signal comparison
- Skills must be treated as production code with proper ownership, testing, and lifecycle management
- Enterprise AI scaling requires connecting AI platforms to organizational capabilities and context through structured governance
